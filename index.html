Crosscutting Concepts

Patterns
Different patterns may be observed at each of the scales at which a system is studied and can provide evidence for causality in explanations of phenomena.
Lesson 2.3 Alignment: Students observe patterns at multiple scales: letter frequency patterns (Stage 2), word patterns (Stage 3), structural patterns (Stage 5-6), and meta-patterns in how learning itself progresses.
Systems and System Models
Models can be used to predict the behavior of a system.
Lesson 2.3 Alignment: Students develop mental models of AI learning systems by observing how inputs (prompts) produce systematically different outputs depending on learning stage.
Stability and Change
Change and rates of change can be quantified and modeled over very short or very long periods of time.
Lesson 2.3 Alignment: Students document how AI capabilities change across learning stages, observing both gradual improvements (Stage 1‚Üí2‚Üí3) and more dramatic leaps (Stage 4‚Üí5).
